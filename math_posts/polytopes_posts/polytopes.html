Polytopes from Group Theory

Definition. The Euclidean plane is the set \(\mathbb{R}^n\), endowed with the metric \(d\), which is defined for any two points \(\mathbf{p} = (p_1,\dots, p_n), \mathbf{q} = (q_1,\dots, q_n), \) as \[d(\mathbf{p},\mathbf{q}) = \sqrt{\sum_{i = 1}^n (p_i - q_i)^2}.\] This metric space is denoted as \(\mathbb{E}^n = (\mathbb{R}^n , d)\)
