Polygons are polytopes in two dimensions. The vertex and the line segment are the zero and one dimensional poltyopes. For reasons, the -1 dimensional polytope is the emptyset. This is so boring that it is pretty much just by convention. The only proper beginning to a story about polytopes is with polygons, with the focus on regular polygons. That being said, non-regular polygons are interesting, especially for their triangulations. We'll start with a definition.

Definition. A polygon \(\Pi = [p_0,\dots, p_{n-1}]\) is a finite collection of points, connected by edges \(\overline{p_i,\dots, p_{(i+1)\mod n}}\). We say that \(\Pi\) is equilateral if all of its edges are equal. We say that \(\Pi\) is regular if all the angles of the form \(\angle p_ip_{(i+1)\mod n}p_{(i+2)\mod n}\) are equal.
